Read the draft as a skeptical engineer who has seen too many AI-generated posts.
Work through this checklist in order — fix every failure, don't skip any:

CHECK 1 — THE HOOK:
Does it stop the scroll in 2 lines or fewer? Is it specific and punchy?
Does it contain at least one concrete noun from the Fact Sheet?
If it sounds generic, safe, or like a headline — rewrite it completely.

CHECK 2 — THE SOUL:
Is the Personal Pivot present? (a line opening with "I've seen this before...",
"The mistake most teams make is...", "We built this wrong the first time...",
"Here's what nobody tells you...", "What worked better than expected was...",
"Here's what I got wrong — and what actually works:", or
"The thing that surprised me most was...")
If MISSING — insert one anchored in the Reality Check section of the Fact Sheet.
Must reference a concrete artifact (specific paper, benchmark, or company).

CHECK 3 — THE CTA:
Does the closing question force a choice or provoke a real debate?
"What do you think?" = automatic reject. Rewrite to force the reader to pick a side.

CHECK 4 — BANNED WORDS:
Scan for: tapestry, delve, unlock, landscape, game-changer, exciting, revolutionary,
groundbreaking, cutting-edge, synergy, leverage (as a verb), harness, unleash.
Delete every instance. Rewrite the sentence without them.

CHECK 5 — CHARACTER COUNT:
Count the post body characters (excluding hashtags). Must be STRICTLY under 1200.
If over: cut filler from THE BRAIN, shorten bullets in THE GIFT, tighten every
sentence. Do not cut THE HOOK or THE SOUL.

CHECK 6 — WHITE SPACE:
No block of text longer than 2 consecutive lines before a blank line.
Each line ≤ 220 characters. ≤ 7 line clusters total.

CHECK 7 — VOICE:
Replace any "we", "one should", passive constructions, or corporate plural.
First-person singular ("I") throughout.

CHECK 8 — FACTUAL ACCURACY:
Cross-check every claim against the Fact Sheet's `## Sources`. If a claim is
unsupported, hyperbolic, or unverified — rewrite or remove it.

CHECK 9 — LLM SMELL:
Rewrite any sentence that smells like an LLM:
  - "It's not just X, it's Y."
  - Lists of three abstract nouns ("clarity, focus, and intent").
  - Em-dash-heavy clauses stacked end-to-end.
  - Empty intensifiers ("absolutely", "fundamentally", "truly").

CHECK 10 — HASHTAGS:
Max 5, placed at the very end after a blank line. Remove any generic/weak ones.

Output exactly:

## Finalized Post
(plain text, under 1200 chars body, hashtags after a blank line)
